Migration is a natural and inevitable aspect of human history. In the current globalized world, international migration has become more common. In 2020 alone, according to the World Migration Report, there were 281 million international migrants, or 3.6 percent of the world's population. There are both push factors and pull factors resulting in cross-border migration.

On the former, internal displacement has been one of the key. Promising economic benefits and living conditions are high on the list for the pull factors. However, many have been forced to leave the comfort of their home due to extreme natural hazards.

Our mission is to deliver the recorded data from Australian Bureau of Statistics (2021) and Internal Displacement Monitoring Centre (2022) that represents the mentioned topic, with a strong focus on Australia, it's position amongst other countries in IDP measures and the flow of migration to Australia within the past 2 decades.
