Main Page

Migration is a natural and inevitable aspect of human history. In the current globalized world, international migration has become more common. In 2020 alone, according to the World Migration Report, there were 281 million international migrants, or 3.6 percent of the world's population. There are both push factors and pull factors resulting in cross-border migration. On the former, internal displacement has been one of the key. Promising economic benefits and living conditions are high on the list for the pull factors.

Internal Displacement

According to the Global Report on Internal Displacement 2023, over 70 million people were living in internal displacement worldwide at the end of 2022, a 20 percentage increase in a year and the highest number ever recorded. Causes of internal displacement include both natural disasters and socio-economic and political crises. Common among the natural disasters are flood, drought, wildfire, earthquake, and volcanic eruption. Zooming on the case of Australia, wildfire has by far been recorded as the number one case of displacement, followed by storm and earth quake.

Migration Patterns

People from other continents have migrated to Australia. The World Migration Report 20202 from IOM indicates that the country is of the 10 top destinations for international migration. According to the data from the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS), in the year ending 30 June 2022, overseas migration contributed a net gain of 171,000 to Australia's population. This represents a very large increase in net overseas migration on the 2020-21 financial year.
