Internal displacement population migration data provides insights into displacement patterns caused by disasters like floods, earthquakes, mass movements, storms, and wildfires. Analyzing this data helps understand the impacts on different countries. Filtering out countries with over 10,000 displacements allows focus on regions needing immediate attention and support. By ranking countries in different disaster categories based on percentiles, it is possible to identify vulnerable nations and allocate resources effectively.

Analyzing this data reveals countries most affected by specific disasters. By filtering and ranking, policymakers and stakeholders gain insights to implement targeted measures. This data-driven approach improves disaster response, mitigation, and preparedness, protecting vulnerable populations and building resilient communities.
